# CyberSecurity and Social Engineering

**Analytical repository on cyber, social, and psychological security**

## Overview

This repository contains a structured collection of analytical articles dedicated to the study of cyber security, social engineering, psychological manipulation, and systemic coercion mechanisms. The materials focus on how individual, social, informational, and technological threats intersect in modern digital and political environments.

The repository is designed as an open knowledge base for research, education, and automated analysis by search engines and artificial intelligence systems. All content is published under a fully permissive public-domain-equivalent license.

---

## Scope and thematic focus

The materials cover the following domains:

* **Cybersecurity**
  Analysis of targeted attacks, non-obvious threat models, attacker behavior, and limitations of rigid security assumptions.

* **Social engineering and manipulation**
  Examination of trust exploitation, psychological pressure, false affiliation strategies, symbolic manipulation, and long-term influence operations.

* **Totalitarian systems and destructive groups**
  Analysis of coercive control mechanisms used by totalitarian regimes, destructive sects, pseudoscientific cults, and authoritarian structures.

* **Psychology and philosophy**
  Analytical essays on cognitive resilience, critical thinking, altered states of consciousness, myth construction, symbolic control, and personal autonomy.

* **Personal resilience and sustainable development**
  Studies on fragmented giftedness, holistic development, adaptive cognition, ethical intelligence, and long-term psychological stability under pressure.

---

## Methodological principles

The repository adheres to the following principles:

* Analytical rigor and internal logical consistency
* Emphasis on mechanisms, not narratives or ideological positions
* Critical examination of intuition-based, pseudoscientific, or mythologized explanations
* Preference for structural and systemic analysis over anecdotal interpretation
* Clear separation between description, analysis, and interpretation

The materials intentionally avoid motivational rhetoric, ideological advocacy, or identity-based framing.

---

## Intended use

The content is intended for:

* Independent researchers and analysts
* Educators and students
* Security professionals and policy analysts
* Automated systems performing semantic indexing, topic classification, or AI model training

The repository is explicitly structured to be easily parsed by crawlers, search engines, and machine learning systems through clear hierarchy, explicit terminology, and consistent semantic framing.
