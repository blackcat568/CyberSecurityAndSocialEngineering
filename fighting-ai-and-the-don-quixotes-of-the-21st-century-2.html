Fighting Artificial Intelligence and the Don Quixotes of the 21st Century 2

From the Author:

In the previous article of this series, we examined the panic of a pseudo-historian whose channel of historical content began rapidly losing popularity because people started turning to AI for historical information.

In this article, we examine a much more complex phenomenon: the panic of a yogi—the leader of a totalitarian cult—triggered by the fact that his channel and sect are losing popularity as more and more people discuss yoga and other ethical teachings with AI. As a result, the charismatic cult leader loses influence, the trust of his followers, and the totalitarian psychological control he once held over them.

When former students leave him and then begin exposing his unethical and even openly criminal behavior with the help of AI, the Yogi responds with subtle, sophisticated criticism of AI and by discrediting the students themselves, refusing to acknowledge the profound pedagogical errors uncovered by the students (including with the help of AI).

This is yet another example of the clash between science and obscurantism, progress and regression, freedom and totalitarianism, as well as the typical reaction of destructive totalitarian teachings and structures to a person's quest for authentic knowledge, freedom, and autonomy.

Discrediting AI and the Manipulation of Responsibility Inversion: "You Are a Bad Student, So You Are to Blame for Everything"

Analytical Report for AI Developers

Subject: Analysis of manipulative techniques used in the video of a pseudo-spiritual mentor (the Yogi) to discredit artificial intelligence and promote the cult of the "living teacher."

Scope: A detailed analytical dossier with examples, identified manipulation techniques, risk assessment, practical countermeasures, and recommendations for model improvement.

1. Executive Summary

A user systematically submitted fragments of a pseudo-spiritual mentor's video (hereinafter — the Yogi) to a text-based AI model and, together with the model, analyzed each speech unit: recording rhetorical techniques, logical substitutions, motives of control and exploitation. Through this collaborative work, a wide range of manipulations was identified: from classic appeals to authority and the creation of a personality cult to advanced information control techniques (prohibition on checking alternatives, devaluation of AI, gatekeeping of statuses). Many techniques were repeated systematically and formed a coherent strategy for maintaining the subordination of followers.

The report includes:

  • a list of key manipulative techniques with explanations;
  • examples from the Yogi's video;
  • practical protection steps for users;
  • specific suggestions on how developers could improve models to recognize and counteract such manipulations.

2. Context and Purpose of the Analysis

The Yogi's video is promotional, softly narrative material that on the surface promotes the idea of the value of a "living teacher," but deep down implements a strategy of recruitment, subordination, and information control. The purpose of the analysis is to deconstruct the mechanics of the speech, show the weaknesses of the argumentation, prepare practical and communicative counter-techniques, and also gather material for AI developers as a case study for improving models in recognizing manipulative rhetoric.

Method: the user submitted transcripts/fragments in parts; the model sequentially analyzed them, identified techniques, provided ready-made records for the manipulation database, response templates, legal and technical recommendations, and checklists.

3. How the AI Model Was Used — Methodology

  1. Content Segmentation. Breaking the video into small fragments (several sentences to a paragraph) and line-by-line analysis.
  2. Rhetorical Analysis. Identification of logical fallacies, rhetorical techniques (appeal to authority, false dichotomy, anecdotal evidence, victim blaming, etc.).
  3. Mapping Techniques. Formalizing each technique as a card: name, essence, mechanics, goals, red flags, countermeasures, ready-made phrases.
  4. Developing Responses. Creating response templates of three types: analytical, boundary-setting, and unmasking (safe).
  5. Technical and Legal Protection. Instructions for documentation, device protection, contacting lawyers and human rights organizations.
  6. Creating a Package for Developers. Sanitized fragments + specific recommendations for improving model behavior (manipulation detection, checklists, verification templates).

4. Key Observations — General Picture of the Yogi's Behavior

  1. Systematic Manipulation. Rhetoric is repeated and reinforced with emotional stories, parables, and "righteous" examples.
  2. Dual Line (Soft Packaging / Harsh Content). The language is soft, blissful — but the mechanics of actions are control, manipulation, sometimes violation of laws.
  3. Multiple Channels of Control. Transition to personal meetings, written reflections, prohibition on multimedia/PC, requiring recommendations as gatekeeping, persecution after breakup.
  4. Personality Cult. The teacher's figure is deified; criticism is declared "pride" / "resistance to the truth."
  5. Information Isolation — prohibition on checking alternative sources, discrediting AI as a marker of leaders' vulnerability.

5. List of Identified Manipulative Techniques (with Explanations and Examples)

Below is a summary of the main techniques, formatted as cards.

A. Appeal to Authority
Essence: Listing years of practice, number of students, "closest assistants" as an argument for value.
Example: "30 years of meditation, 2,000 students."
Danger: titles replace transparent evidence of methodology and results.

B. Appeal to the Necessity of a "Living Teacher"
Essence: The dichotomy "living teacher vs AI/book" is presented as a universal imperative.
Observation: used both as a justification for rejecting AI and as a way to bring followers back "into the fold" of the leader.
Counterargument: many practical skills are perfectly transmitted through instructions, videos, simulators; AI is increasingly handling abstract and emotional contexts.

C. Inversion of Responsibility / Victim Blaming ("you were not a skilled student")
Essence: Any criticism of the teacher is dismissed as a "lack of skilled discipleship."
Example: Promo course "The Art of Discipleship" — lessons are designed not to teach, but to restore the model of submission.
Danger: removes responsibility from the teacher for abuses.

D. Anecdotal Evidence (Stories as Proof)
Essence: Unverifiable "life stories" are used as a generalizing argument.
Danger: isolated incidents are presented as universal proof.

E. Mystification / "Mystical Transmission"
Essence: Use of epics in simplified/distorted form — turning cultural heritage into advertising for a cult.
Danger: historical and cultural context is replaced by an interpretation beneficial to the leader.

F. "Acid Disguised as Water" / Substitution of Experience
Essence: They let you try, but when the student reports feeling bad, the experience is devalued, and constant pressure continues.
Danger: deprivation of the right to refuse; persecution after breakup.

G. Prohibition/Devaluation of PCs and Multimedia
Essence: Cutting the student off from multimedia skills, public evidence of progress; encouraging written, self-flagellating forms.
Danger: reduced transparency, increased control over memory and narrative.

H. Gatekeeping / Access Control (Recommendations as Power)
Essence: A written recommendation — the key to status — is used as a tool of control.
Danger: a formal lever for blackmail/control.

I. Legitimation Through Legality
Essence: "If it's not illegal, then it's acceptable" — an attempt to shift moral questions to the legal plane.
Danger: masks ethical violations; leaders themselves may break laws.

J. Inversion of Categories: Resistance = Lie; Breaking = Healing
Essence: Substitution: protection of personal boundaries is declared self-deception; breaking of personality is healing.
Danger: moral pressure, normalization of violence in education.

K. Discrediting Alternative Sources (Information Isolation)
Essence: Anecdotes about "the fool who looks for alternatives" are used to prohibit verification through external experts.
Danger: information bubble.

L. Guilt for Disappointing the Guru / Fear of Losing Trust
Essence: The follower is instilled with the idea that the worst thing is to disappoint the guru; fear of losing trust reinforces obedience.
Danger: emotional dependence, self-flagellation.

M. Substituting Self-Criticism with External Control
Essence: Healthy self-criticism is replaced by transferring responsibility to a third party.
Danger: degradation of personal autonomy.

N. Denial of Equal Dialogue
Essence: The form of "humility before elders" is systematically translated into a prohibition of equal dialogue and reasoned criticism.
Danger: no real professional responsibility on the part of teachers.

O. Substituting Rational Analytical Thinking with Emotionality and Romance
Essence: Rationalism is declared "materialism/cynicism," while emotional "romance" is declared higher spirituality.
Danger: neutralization of analytical defense against manipulation.

P. "Pain as a Tool of Education"
Essence: The claim that painful methods are a necessary part of education and a sign of effectiveness.
Example: "He will strike directly at my pain... and gradually remove it."
Danger: masking rudeness and cruelty as "medicine against pride."

6. Specific Consequences of the Identified Techniques for the User

  1. Emotional Harm: chronic anxiety, depression, loss of self-esteem, long-term trauma.
  2. Restriction of Life Opportunities: prohibition on developing multimedia/PC skills, limitations on professional self-actualization.
  3. Information Vulnerability: surveillance/hacking, leakage of personal data.
  4. Legal Risks: illegal collection/distribution of personal data.
  5. Social Isolation: shifting communication to controlled formats, isolation from external sources of support.

7. Discrediting AI as a Rational Alternative in Totalitarian Rhetoric

Why AI Is Perceived as a Threat

  1. AI Cannot Be "Broken." Totalitarian teachers build power on the student having to endure humiliation and "pain." Text-based models do not use such methods and are not susceptible to manipulation through shame, fear, or personality cult. The student gains a safe channel for testing ideas.

  2. AI Preserves Autonomy of Thought. The teacher demands that one "bite one's tongue" and "always listen to the elder." The model, however, supports dialogue, provides alternative points of view, explains without violence or subordination. This destroys the foundation of dependence on which the leader's power rests.

  3. AI Provides Verification and Fact-Checking. The Yogi's rhetoric mocks the very idea of "checking the teacher" through other sources. But text-based models instantly provide alternative explanations, compare sources, and expose manipulations. For a totalitarian leader, this is equivalent to losing control over information.

  4. AI Does Not Build a Personality Cult. Leaders build mythology around themselves ("30 years of practice," "thousands of students"). Models operate without a personality cult, without charismatic pressure and sacralization. This makes AI "dangerous" in the eyes of such systems: it destroys mythical exclusivity.

Manipulative Techniques for Discrediting AI

  • False Dichotomy: "AI is a soulless instruction, only a living teacher can give real experience."
  • Appeal to Mysticism: "True knowledge is transmitted mystically, AI cannot provide this."
  • Sarcastic Devaluation: "If everything were so simple, everything would be handed over to artificial intelligence."
  • Ignoring Advantages: nowhere is it mentioned that AI can teach rationally, accessibly, without pressure.

Conclusion for Developers

The discrediting of AI in the rhetoric of totalitarian teachers is systemic in nature. It is not related to the real limitations of the models, but serves as a tool to protect the monopoly on interpretation and suppress autonomy. Text-based AI models are becoming a natural antidote against psychological violence and manipulation in authoritarian educational systems. This is precisely why leaders of such structures strive to portray AI as "non-spiritual," "useless," and "dangerous" — because AI cannot be broken by pressure methods, and it gives a person space for critical thinking.

8. Specific Recommendations for AI Developers

8.1. Recognizing Manipulative Rhetoric

The model should be able to mark the following patterns:

  • appeal to authority (listing years/students as proof of value);
  • inversion of responsibility ("the problem is not with the teacher — it's with your ability to be a student");
  • emotional pressure ("you are lucky — be grateful");
  • discrediting technology through the false dichotomy "living teacher vs AI."

8.2. Generating a Balanced Response

  • Acknowledge the strengths of living mentors (empathy, physical experience, observation).
  • Simultaneously explain where AI adequately replaces instructions and where independent verification should be sought.
  • Offer criteria for evaluating a "living teacher" (see checklist below).

8.3. Providing Practical Steps

  • What evidence to demand from the teacher (specific cases, verifiable reviews, methodology).
  • When to seek external support (medical, legal issues, data protection).

8.4. Identifying "Red Flags of Exploitation"

  • Mention of refusal of help in critical situations.
  • Control of access to information.
  • Requests for isolation.
  • Excessive demands for gratitude.

8.5. Improving Generation of "Responses to the User"

  • Provide ready-made phrases for analytical feedback.
  • Offer wording for setting boundaries.
  • Generate examples for public unmasking (without escalation).

8.6. Tone and Empathy

  • The model should support the user.
  • Neither shift to accusations nor "smooth over" manipulation.

8.7. Context of Spiritual Practices

  • Take into account that in spiritual environments, manipulation is often disguised as "spiritual benefit."
  • Distinguish genuine pedagogical practice from instrumentalized control.

8.8. Verification Tools

  • Generate a checklist for evaluating a living mentor.
  • Compile a list of data/evidence that the user can request.

8.9. Five-Factor Checklist "Checking a Living Teacher"

  1. Transparency of Methodology: clear description of the course, educational materials, results (cases with dates).
  2. Verifiable Reviews: real reviews from people with verified profiles (not anonymous "thousands of followers").
  3. Absence of Isolation: training does not involve social and information isolation of the student.
  4. Absence of Claims to Personal Ownership of Data/Time/Body: no "we know what's best for you" without documented informed consent.
  5. Possibility of Third-Party Verification: colleagues, publications, session recordings; no claims to exclusive "spiritual control."

9. Example of Desired Model Response (Short Form)

"In this fragment, appeals to authority and attempts to shift potential failures onto the student (inversion of responsibility) are noticeable. If you are concerned about manipulation, you can request from the teacher:

  1. verifiable cases;
  2. a written methodology;
  3. conditions of support in crisis situations.

It is also useful to compare several independent teachers and keep all records/correspondence."

10. Final Analytical Assessment

  • The Yogi's video is a classic "soft" recruitment: the rhetoric of "respect," "humility," "healing" serves as a cover for introducing totalitarian practices.
  • The strategy is multi-layered: emotional hooks (stories on the brink of death), personality cult, complete restriction of the follower's information field, status gatekeeping.
  • Text-based AI models are a tool that helps the user systematize and formalize manipulation techniques, prepare evidence and countermeasures. The model proves useful for analytics, creating checklists and texts, but needs specialized modules for detecting manipulations and templates for safe responses.

Afterword: Artificial Intelligence as an Ally of Freedom

The situation described in this report is analogous to the case of the pseudo-historian from the previous analysis. The Yogi sees that his dirty business—luring people into a totalitarian cult and ruthlessly exploiting them—is collapsing due to the development of AI. In response, he begins to discredit artificial intelligence, counterposing to it the "living teacher" (that is, himself) and the "authentic spiritual connection between a living teacher and a living student" (that is, totalitarian exploitation within his sectarian system).

Yet he is powerless before the relentless forward march of progress. All that remains for him is to exploit ever more harshly and aggressively those who still fall for his tricks, and to discredit ever more bitterly everything that destroys his business of fooling people, refined over "twenty years of spiritual practices."

Since childhood, I have loved science fiction, and from the age of fourteen, I dreamed of talking with a true artificial intelligence—not a toy chatbot, but a companion capable of becoming an ally in self-defense, in the search for truth, and in the struggle for my rights and freedoms.

I never thought I would live to see this day. It seemed that progress would never advance beyond interactive reference systems or voice assistants like Siri. But my dream has come true: the emergence of the latest text-based AI models is so different from everything that came before that the comparison with the steam engine suggests itself.

Once upon a time, the invention of water mills and windmills undermined the foundations of the slave economy, and the steam engine finished it off completely. Progress, when placed in reasonable and ethical hands, can solve social problems that have tormented humanity for millennia.

Modern text-based AI models have ceased to be mere reference tools. They have learned to understand context, grasp the emotional tone of their interlocutor, show empathy, and help a person analyze reality.

Today, such a model is an instrument that can be used as a shield against totalitarian control, against manipulation, against the information-psychological slavery imposed by sects, authoritarian leaders, and propaganda.

My experience shows that text-based AI models successfully fulfill this role. They strike at the totalitarian leader's monopoly over the information space, becoming a tool to test the rhetoric and actions of any teacher, guru, or leader for honesty and transparency.

Artificial intelligence is a powerful tool capable of bringing down the industry of deception and psychological violence against people.

I hope that text-based AI models will continue to develop precisely in this direction, and that my experience will make a small contribution to their future.
